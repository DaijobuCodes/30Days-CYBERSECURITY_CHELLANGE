Reset Your Advertising ID for Better Privacy

Advertising IDs are unique identifiers used by companies to track your activity and deliver targeted ads. Resetting your Advertising ID can help protect your privacy by giving you a fresh start and reducing the accuracy of ad tracking.

Real-Life Example of Advertising ID Misuse

In 2020, a popular app was found to be collecting users' Advertising IDs without proper consent and combining it with other tracking data to create detailed profiles. This raised significant privacy concerns as users were unknowingly being tracked across multiple apps and platforms. The company faced legal actions and had to pay fines due to privacy violations.

Why Resetting Your Advertising ID is Important

Failing to reset your Advertising ID can expose you to the following risks:

  • Extensive tracking across multiple apps and platforms.
  • Targeted ads based on a detailed profile created without your knowledge.
  • Loss of privacy and potential data breaches.
  • Reduced control over who has access to your data.

Steps to Reset Advertising ID

Instructions: Go to Settings > Privacy > General > Turn off “Let apps use advertising ID” > Click on "Reset ID" to reset your advertising ID.

Instructions: Go to Settings > Google > Ads > Tap on "Reset Advertising ID". You can also toggle off "Opt out of Ads Personalization" for further privacy.

Instructions: Go to Settings > Privacy > Tracking > Toggle off "Allow Apps to Request to Track" and tap on "Reset Identifier" for a fresh Advertising ID.
